A "Cybersecurity Asset" represents an abstracted feature or functionality that is inherently part of a TOE, which needs to be protected by security measures to keep the TOE working as intended.<br/>This element strongly relates to the "cybersecurity property" in ISO 21434, which is generally defined as an attribute that can be worth protecting. Its impact on systems health can be rated via the CIA triad (confidentiality, integrity, availability).<br/><br/>

Attribute Scope Type
Availability Public ThreeLevelImpactScale
Notes: Availability Rating is a measure used to assess the importance of a system, service, or data being accessible and operational when needed. It helps organizations determine the required uptime, redundancy, and recovery strategies to ensure that critical assets remain available to authorized users, even in the face of failures, cyberattacks, or disasters.<br/>
Confidentiality Public ThreeLevelImpactScale
Notes: Confidentiality Rating refers to the evaluation or classification of information based on its level of sensitivity and the required level of protection to prevent unauthorized access or disclosure. The Confidentiality Rating determines how strictly access to the information should be controlled, ensuring that only authorized individuals or systems can view or modify it.<br/>
Integrity Public ThreeLevelImpactScale
Notes: Integrity Rating is a measure used to assess the importance of maintaining the accuracy, consistency, and trustworthiness of data and systems. It helps determine the level of protection needed to prevent unauthorized modification, tampering, or corruption of critical information, whether accidental or malicious.<br/>
