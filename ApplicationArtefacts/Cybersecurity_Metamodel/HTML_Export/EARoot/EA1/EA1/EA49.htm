Risk Rating is an assessment of the overall risk associated with a potential failure mode, based on its Severity (S), Occurrence (O), and Detection (D) ratings. It is used to prioritize corrective actions and improve system reliability.<br/>

Attribute Scope Type
Detection Public Detection Scale
Notes: Detection Rating (D) represents the likelihood that a failure mode or its cause will be detected before reaching the customer or the next process step. It is part of the Risk Priority Number (RPN) calculation and the Action Priority (AP) assessment in FMEA.<br/>
Occurrence Public Occurrence Scale
Notes: Occurrence Rating (O) represents the likelihood or frequency of a failure cause occurring during the design or manufacturing process.<br/>
Risk Priority Number Protected int
Notes: Risk Priority Number (RPN) is a numerical value used in Failure Modes and Effects Analysis (FMEA) to prioritize risks. It is calculated as positive integer and normed to fit into the range 1...100:<br/><br/>RPN = Severity × Occurrence × Detection x (100/1000)<br/><br/>Where:<br/>    Severity (S): Impact of the failure [1-10]<br/>    Occurrence (O): Likelihood of failure happening [1-10]<br/>    Detection (D): Likelihood of detecting the failure before it occurs [1-10]<br/><br/>Higher RPN values indicate higher-priority risks requiring mitigation.<br/>Risk Ratings featuring the RiskRatingType "Residual Risk Rating" with high RPN value should be well thought if to accept or apply further countermeasures to reduce the RPN.<br/>
